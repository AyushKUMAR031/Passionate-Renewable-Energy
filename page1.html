Renewable Energy!

{ I }

Explaination

Renewable energy refers to energy that comes from sources that are naturally replenished and can be sustained over time. Common examples include solar, wind, hydro, geothermal, and biomass energy.

Renewable energy sources are abundant and widely available. For example, the sun provides more energy in a single hour than the entire world consumes in a year.

{ II }

How it Helps nature.

Renewable energy sources do not produce greenhouse gas emissions or other harmful pollutants, making them a clean source of energy that can help mitigate the impacts of climate change.

Renewable energy can help enhance energy security by reducing dependence on foreign oil and other non-renewable sources of energy.

{ III }

Economic Benefit.

The use of renewable energy can create new jobs and economic opportunities in areas such as manufacturing, installation, and maintenance of renewable energy systems.

Renewable energy sources can increase access to energy in rural and remote areas, helping to promote social and economic development in these regions.
